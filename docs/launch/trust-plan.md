# Cloudrix Trust Plan — 60 Days from Zero Reputation to Verifiable Proof

**Context.** Cloudrix is a solo-founder Dutch studio (founded 2026, KVK 97732699, Tilburg). Flagship: EU AI Act implementation engineering — Quick Scan €2,500 → programs €25–60K. Our buyers are diligence-minded compliance and engineering leaders. They will check every claim. Therefore: **no claim goes on the site before it is verifiable.** The /case-studies page now serves clearly-labeled Reference Scenarios; this plan is how we replace them with real, named proof.

**Operating principle:** honesty *is* the positioning. Every trust asset below must survive the question "can a skeptical buyer verify this in 60 seconds?"

## Phase 1 — Days 1–14: Verifiable identity + interim guarantees

These cost nothing and are checkable immediately.

### 1.1 Verifiable signals (ship this week)
- [ ] **KVK link in the footer and /about**: link "KVK 97732699" directly to the kvk.nl register entry (`https://www.kvk.nl/bestellen/#/97732699`). A compliance buyer's first check.
- [ ] **Founder LinkedIn**: link Firas's personal LinkedIn from /about, /testimonials, and the site footer. A real person with a real work history beats any anonymous "team."
- [ ] **GitHub org** (`github.com/cloudrix` or similar): create it now even if near-empty; pin the open-source repo from Phase 2 as soon as it exists. Public commit history = proof of engineering competence.
- [ ] **Google Business Profile** (Tilburg address): create now so reviews have somewhere to land after the first engagements.

### 1.2 Interim proof: guarantees instead of testimonials (ship this week)
Until we have named clients, we substitute *risk transfer* for social proof. Publish on /testimonials and /case-studies:
- **24-hour response guarantee** on every inquiry (business days, CET) — measured and stated plainly.
- **Quick Scan money-back guarantee**: if the €2,500 Quick Scan doesn't identify at least one concrete, actionable compliance gap, full refund. Cheap for us, decisive for a skeptical buyer.
- **No-lock-in clause**: all deliverables (docs, code, IaC) are the client's property from day one; published in plain language.
- **NDA-first**: we sign the client's NDA before any sensitive discussion.

### 1.3 Pilot-client offer (start outreach day 3)
Target: **1–3 pilot clients** in 60 days at case-study pricing.

- **The offer**: 30–40% off list price (e.g., Quick Scan €1,500 instead of €2,500; program discounts negotiated per scope), founder-led delivery, in exchange for: (a) a named, attributable testimonial if — and only if — they're satisfied; (b) a written case study they approve before publication; (c) permission to use their logo.

## Phase 2 — Days 7–35: Publish-to-prove content

We can't show past clients, but we can show work product publicly. Everything here doubles as top-of-funnel.

- [ ] **Open-source EU AI Act checker** (days 7–25): a CLI/web tool that walks a team through risk-classification of their AI systems (Annex III mapping, prohibited-practice screening, provider/deployer role check) and outputs a structured report. MIT license, on the GitHub org, linked from /resources. This is the single highest-leverage asset: it proves both engineering skill and domain depth, and every user is a lead.
- [ ] **Public compliance-readiness checklist** (day 10): a genuinely useful, ungated EU AI Act readiness checklist page (Article 11 docs, logging, human oversight, FRIA triggers, Dec 2027 timeline). Gate only the downloadable/spreadsheet version behind email.
- [ ] **Teardown posts** (2 in 60 days): "We assessed {well-known public AI product} against the EU AI Act — here's what we found" using only public information. Concrete, technical, opinionated. Publish on the blog, syndicate to LinkedIn and dev.to; submit the tool as Show HN.
- [ ] **Build-in-public cadence**: 1–2 LinkedIn posts/week from the founder account (tool progress, AI Act interpretation notes, honest notes on starting a studio). The honesty angle — "we deleted our fake case studies" — is itself a strong post.

## Phase 3 — Days 30–60: Convert pilots into named proof

- [ ] **Day 30–45**: deliver first pilot Quick Scan. Over-deliver: findings report + prioritized remediation roadmap + 30-min follow-up call at no charge.
- [ ] **On completion**: same-week ask, while goodwill is highest — (1) named testimonial (offer to draft 2–3 options they can edit/approve), (2) LinkedIn recommendation on the founder profile, (3) Google review, (4) case-study interview (30 min).
- [ ] **Replace one Reference Scenario at a time** with the real case study; keep remaining scenarios labeled as they are now. Real case studies state only client-approved facts and metrics.
- [ ] **Third-party validation path**:
  - **Clutch profile** (day 30): create it; Clutch phone-verifies reviews, which diligence-minded buyers know. Ask each pilot client for a Clutch review — 2 verified reviews beats 20 site quotes.
  - **Trustpilot**: defer until ~5 clients (an empty/thin Trustpilot page hurts more than none). Revisit day 90.
  - **Partner adjacency**: get listed in NL AI ecosystem directories (NL AI Coalitie, local startup hubs) — checkable third-party mentions.

## Standing rules (never expire)
1. No metric on the site we cannot document on request.
2. No anonymous testimonials, ever — guarantees instead.
3. Every real case study is client-approved in writing before publication.
4. Reference Scenarios stay clearly labeled until fully replaced by real ones.
5. If a guarantee is triggered (refund, missed response time), we honor it without argument — and it's cheap marketing when we do.
