## First comment from the maker

> Hi Product Hunt — I'm Firas, a senior software engineer from Tilburg, Netherlands. This is a solo project.
>
> Why I built it: last month the EU finalized the "Digital Omnibus," which moved the AI Act's high-risk compliance deadline from August 2026 to December 2, 2027 (and to 2028 for AI embedded in regulated products). Half the teams I talk to concluded "great, we can stop thinking about it." That's wrong in two ways: the prohibitions and AI-literacy duties have applied since February 2025, most transparency obligations still start August 2026 — and the high-risk requirements didn't get easier, you just got more runway to engineer them properly.
>
> But the first question is always the same: *does any of this even apply to us, and at what risk tier?* Answering that today means reading the Act or paying a law firm. So I built a scanner: you describe your AI system (purpose, domain, who it affects, how decisions are made), and it maps that to the Act's risk categories and tells you which obligations apply and on what date — post-Omnibus dates, not the outdated ones most content still cites.
>
> Honest limitations: it's a structured self-assessment, not legal advice, and edge cases (Annex I embedded systems, GPAI providers) still need a human. I'm that human for the hard cases — that's the business model: the scanner is free forever, and I sell implementation engineering to the subset who need it. No email wall on the scanner, because I hate those too.
>
> I'd genuinely like feedback on the classification questions — if you run your system through it and the result feels wrong, tell me why. That's the most valuable comment you can leave.
